## 2. Spec-Driven AI-Assisted Development
### Summary
Spec-driven development (SDD) is gaining traction as a methodology that emphasizes the creation of specifications before coding. This approach aims to enhance collaboration between human developers and AI agents, ensuring that the generated code aligns with predefined requirements.

### Key Findings
- **Definition and Implementation**: SDD involves writing a detailed spec that serves as the source of truth for both developers and AI agents. Tools like Kiro and Spec-kit are being explored for their effectiveness in implementing SDD.
- **Challenges**: Evaluating SDD tools can be time-consuming, and there are concerns about their applicability to real-world coding problems, especially in existing codebases.

### Sources
- [Understanding Spec-Driven Development](https://martinfowler.com/articles/exploring-gen-ai/sdd-3-tools.html)

## 3. Edge AI
### Summary
Edge AI refers to the deployment of AI algorithms on local devices rather than relying on cloud-based processing. This approach reduces latency and bandwidth usage, making it suitable for real-time applications.

### Key Findings
- **Applications**: Edge AI is being integrated into various applications, from smart devices to autonomous vehicles, enhancing their capabilities without the need for constant cloud connectivity.
- **Future Trends**: As AI models become more efficient, the trend towards edge computing is expected to grow, enabling more sophisticated AI applications in real-time environments.
